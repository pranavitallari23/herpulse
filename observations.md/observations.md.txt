# HerPulse - Exploratory Data Analysis Findings

## Dataset Overview

* Dataset contains 541 patient records and 45 features.
* The target variable is **PCOS (Y/N)** indicating whether a patient has PCOS.

## PCOS Distribution

* 364 records belong to women without PCOS.
* 177 records belong to women with PCOS.

## BMI Analysis

* Women diagnosed with PCOS have a higher average BMI compared to women without PCOS.

## Weight Analysis

* Women with PCOS show a higher average body weight than women without PCOS.

## Correlation Analysis

The features most strongly associated with PCOS are:

1. Follicle Number (Right Ovary)
2. Follicle Number (Left Ovary)
3. Skin Darkening
4. Hair Growth
5. Weight Gain
6. Irregular Menstrual Cycles
7. Fast Food Consumption

## Key Insights

* Follicle count appears to be the strongest indicator of PCOS in this dataset.
* Physical symptoms such as skin darkening and excessive hair growth show strong associations with PCOS.
* Lifestyle-related factors such as weight gain and fast food consumption also demonstrate notable relationships.
* Women with PCOS tend to have higher average BMI and weight compared to women without PCOS.

Day 4 - Data Preprocessing

- Dataset initially contained 45 columns.
- Removed Unnamed: 44 due to excessive missing values.
- Removed Sl. No and Patient File No. as identifier columns.
- No duplicate records were found.
- Filled missing value in Marraige Status (Yrs) using median.
- Filled missing value in Fast food (Y/N) using mode.
- Saved cleaned dataset with 541 records and 42 features.
## Feature Importance Analysis

The Random Forest model identified the following as the most influential features for predicting PCOS:

1. Follicle No. (R)
2. Follicle No. (L)
3. Skin darkening (Y/N)
4. Weight gain (Y/N)
5. Hair growth (Y/N)
6. AMH (ng/mL)
7. Cycle (R/I)
8. LH (mIU/mL)
9. Cycle length (days)
10. Age (yrs)

Observation:
Follicle count in both ovaries was the strongest predictor of PCOS. Clinical symptoms such as skin darkening, weight gain, and hair growth also showed significant importance. Hormonal indicators such as AMH and LH contributed to the model's predictions.

## Model Evaluation

Accuracy: 87.16%

The Random Forest Classifier performed well on the PCOS dataset.

Key metrics:
- Accuracy: 87.16%

The model successfully identified important clinical and hormonal indicators associated with PCOS.
